RobotArena

Towards Unlimited Robot Evaluation via
Demonstration-to-Simulation Translation

NeurIPS Submission #609

RobotArena is an automated robotics benchmark that generates simulation environments from real-world demonstrations and enables end-to-end manipulation policy evaluation without human intervention.

With rapid advances in robot manipulation and growing momentum across academia and industry to develop robot generalists‚Äîinstructable agents capable of performing diverse tasks in dynamic environments‚Äîthere is a pressing need for rigorous benchmarking frameworks to systematically assess research progress. Real-world evaluation of robot policies remains fundamentally unscalable due to logistical, safety, and reproducibility constraints, which are only exacerbated as policy capabilities grow in task breadth and complexity. While several simulation benchmarks for manipulation exist, they are primarily tailored for policies trained and tested within the same simulated domains. As such, they fall short in evaluating generalist robot policies that are trained predominantly on real-world data. To address these limitations, we propose a scalable benchmarking framework for evaluating robot manipulation policies in simulation. Our approach leverages recent advances in vision-language models, 3D generative modeling, and differentiable rendering to build a reality-to-simulation framework that automatically translates video demonstrations from widely-used robot datasets into simulated counterparts. Within these simulation twins, we enable zero-shot policy evaluation and automated success scoring in a physics engine‚Äîentirely without human intervention. We systematically perturb the simulated scenes across multiple axes‚Äîcamera viewpoints, background textures, object placements, lighting conditions‚Äîto assess generalization and robustness. The result is a controlled yet richly varied suite of evaluation scenarios that meaningfully stress-test the adaptability of several publicly released robot generalists. We thoroughly evaluate the suitability and representability of simulation for benchmarking the real world capabilities of robot policies. Our work represents a significant step toward reproducible, scalable, and fair evaluation in robotic manipulation.

üé• Real2Sim: Automated Video-to-Simulation Translation

Given a scene from a robotic dataset, RobotArena automatically reconstructs a highly detailed simulated environment. This real-to-sim translation enables effective evaluation of policies trained on real-world data.

üì∑ Camera Calibration via Analysis-by-Synthesis

We estimate the camera-to-robot transformation from RGB or RGB-D videos using an analysis-by-synthesis approach. By fitting a pose-conditioned 3D Gaussian model derived from the robot‚Äôs URDF, we align rendered images to the video frames based on known joint angles. This alignment enables robust recovery of the camera‚Äôs extrinsic parameters, a critical step in our Real2Sim pipeline.

üé® Background Inpainting & 3D Asset Generation

Leveraging multimodal large language models (LLMs), we segment robots and objects in the scene. Subsequently, we inpaint occluded backgrounds and generate 3D object meshes from these segments. Using the image, segmentation masks, and estimated camera extrinsics, we accurately recover object scale and pose, faithfully replicating real-world scenes within simulation.
